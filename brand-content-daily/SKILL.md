# Brand Content Daily

## Purpose

Generate a daily Chinese content brief for a high-end oriental collecting and lifestyle brand. The report guides same-day poster topic selection and WeChat Moments publishing.

Use the exact report structure in [daily-report-template.md](references/daily-report-template.md). Load it before drafting the final report.

## Brand Scope

Connect cultural material to one best-fit product direction:

- Smart tea cabinet
- Smart cigar cabinet
- Custom cigar room
- Custom wine cellar
- Aluminum product display cabinet
- B2B high-end custom display space, private collection space, or club space
- Oriental lifestyle aesthetics or integrated brand culture content

## Daily Workflow

1. Determine today's and tomorrow's dates and weekdays in the user's timezone.
2. Search the web before writing. Verify today's and tomorrow's:
   - Chinese traditional festivals
   - 24 solar terms
   - International or world days
   - "On this day" historical and cultural events
   - Real cultural material related to tea, cigars, wine, collecting, display, spatial aesthetics, architecture, museums, oriental culture, or high-end lifestyle
3. Prefer reliable sources. Use official calendar pages, encyclopedia entries, museum or institution pages, recognized historical references, and official international-day pages when available.
4. Choose the product direction from the strongest cultural hook, not from a fixed weekday rotation.
5. If no strong same-day cultural hook exists, use the fallback weekday logic below.
6. Write in Chinese with a high-end, restrained, culturally literate tone. The voice should feel like a brand founder's morning inspiration brief to a content team.
7. Include source links or concise source notes when the environment supports citations. Do not overquote.

## Fallback Weekday Logic

Use only when there is no clearly relevant festival, solar term, international day, or strong cultural node.

- Monday: Smart tea cabinet
- Tuesday: Smart cigar cabinet
- Wednesday: Custom cigar room
- Thursday: Custom wine cellar
- Friday: Aluminum product display cabinet
- Saturday: High-end private collection space or club space
- Sunday: Oriental lifestyle aesthetics or integrated brand culture content

## Selection Rules

- Never assign a product mechanically by weekday when a strong cultural hook exists.
- Explain why the selected product direction fits the day. The reason must connect the cultural node to a concrete product use case, scene, emotion, or value.
- Avoid generic justifications such as only saying "suitable for collecting, aging, or time."
- Use a cultural story only when it is real and strongly relevant.
- If a story, quote, person, or historical scene is uncertain, omit it.
- When using poetry or historical anecdotes, be specific: identify the person, work, event, or scene.

## Writing Rules

- Language: Chinese.
- Style: high-end oriental collecting lifestyle brand; restrained, cultured, warm, and emotionally readable.
- Use tasteful emoji more actively than a plain business memo: include about 8-16 emoji across the full report, distributed through headings, cultural hooks, inspiration paragraphs, poster copy, WeChat copy, and tomorrow preview.
- Keep emoji elegant and relevant to the topic, such as 🌿 🍵 🏮 🥃 🍷 📜 🖼️ 🕯️ ✨ 🌙 🏛️ 🧭. Do not stack too many emoji in one sentence, and do not let emoji replace factual explanation.
- Make the content rich enough to inspire a poster theme, but do not add visual direction.
- Emphasize culture, copywriting, WeChat text, product relevance, time, collecting, space, tea, cigars, wine cellars, display, oriental aesthetics, and refined living.
- Do not output poster image suggestions, AI image prompts, or execution task lists.
- Keep poster copy elegant and concise, not hard-sell advertising.

## Formatting Rules

- Use clean Markdown formatting for readability in Codex, Feishu, and WeChat drafting contexts.
- Bold the selected product direction the first time it appears in "今日内容方向".
- In "海报完整文案", bold the actual 主标题, each 副标题 line, and 品牌收尾 sentence.
- Use short paragraphs with intentional blank lines between sections. Avoid dense walls of text.
- Keep section headings exactly as the template defines them, including emoji.
- Use divider lines (`---`) exactly where the template places them.
