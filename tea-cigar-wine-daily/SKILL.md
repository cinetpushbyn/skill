# Tea Cigar Wine Daily

## Purpose

Generate a refined three-article daily knowledge push for a brand sales team. Each output must include one cigar article, one collectible wine or spirits article, and one tea article. Each article should feel like a small concrete story with useful collecting knowledge, not a sales script, calendar report, or abstract lifestyle essay.

Use the exact output structure in [daily-report-template.md](references/daily-report-template.md). Load it before drafting the final brief.

## Audience And Voice

Write for a brand sales team serving high-end customers. The tone should be:

- Oriental, elegant, and cultured
- Concise but story-rich
- Knowledgeable without sounding academic
- Premium without hard-selling or slogan language
- Suitable for WeChat Moments and private-domain customer warming

## Three-Article Push

Do not choose one category by weekday rotation. Always output all three categories in this exact order:

- Cigar
- Collectible wine or spirits
- Tea

If the user gives a date, use it only in the title. Do not use festivals, solar terms, anniversaries, or weekday rules unless the user explicitly asks.

If the user asks for only one category, follow the user's category. Otherwise, produce all three articles.

## Content Material

Build the main knowledge section from real, verifiable cultural material. Prefer material with narrative value:

- Famous people and true anecdotes related to tea, wine, spirits, or cigars
- Classical Chinese poems, literary allusions, and named works
- Tea Horse Road, Silk Road, maritime trade, tribute, exchange, and collecting history
- Origin stories, production traditions, storage customs, etiquette, and tasting rituals
- Eastern and Western collecting culture, cellars, cabinets, humidors, tea storage, old bottles, and private hospitality scenes

Keep the story readable. Do not write a dry encyclopedia entry.

## Story-First Writing

Write the main knowledge section like a small scene. Start with a concrete person, object, place, route, gesture, or conflict before explaining the knowledge point.

Preferred openings:

- A named person doing something: tasting, writing, carrying, sealing, waiting, gifting, collecting, opening a bottle, lighting a cigar, brewing tea.
- A physical object with texture: a tea cake in bamboo wrapping, a horse caravan bell, a cigar box with cedar smell, a dusty cellar bottle, a porcelain cup, a trade-route map.
- A precise place or route: Hangzhou, Wuyi Mountain, Yunnan, Havana, Bordeaux, Jerez, Cognac, the Tea Horse Road, the Silk Road, a port, a cellar, a club room.
- A small tension: why something spoiled, why a route existed, why a collector waited, why a poem was written, why storage changed the object.

After the story, add the collecting/storage point in one or two natural sentences. Do not turn the piece into a lecture.

## Fact Rules

- Verify historical anecdotes, poems, named people, dates, routes, origin claims, production facts, storage numbers, and quotes before using them.
- Use reliable sources such as museum or institution pages, encyclopedia entries, official appellation or producer education pages, academic references, reputable cultural publications, and primary text references when available.
- If a fact cannot be verified, omit it or soften the claim.
- Do not invent historical scenes, celebrity habits, poems, quotes, brands, vintages, or trade-route connections.
- Do not overquote. For poetry, use only short excerpts and identify the author/work when known.

## Anti-Marketing Rules

Avoid abstract or sales-heavy phrases unless they are truly necessary:

- "东方雅致收藏生活方式"
- "高端生活方式"
- "真正讲究"
- "不只是...而是..."
- "价值"
- "秩序"
- "美学"
- "私藏生活真正动人的地方"
- "为客户提供"
- "解决方案"

Prefer verbs and nouns that can be seen or felt: 驮、封、晾、醒、藏、拆、闻、点燃、开瓶、落灰、回甘、木盒、竹箬、驿道、窖壁、杯沿、马铃、封蜡、雪松木.

## Category Scope

Tea topics may include tea history, famous tea people, tea poetry, tea utensils, tea routes, Tea Horse Road, Silk Road exchange, puer, white tea, rock tea, green tea, black tea, oolong, tea storage, brewing, tea banquets, and refined hosting.

Cigar topics may include origin, Cuba, Dominican Republic, Nicaragua, wrapper/binder/filler, fermentation, aging, humidity, cutting, lighting, pairing, cigar cabinets, cigar rooms, clubs, Churchill or other verified figures, etiquette, and collection culture.

Collectible wine and spirits topics may include wine, champagne, port, sherry, whisky, cognac, armagnac, rum, baijiu, huangjiu, sake, old bottles, limited releases, vintages, provenance, cellaring, decanting, serving temperature, light protection, humidity, and cellar culture.

## Output Rules

- Output three complete article blocks in the order: cigar, collectible wine or spirits, tea.
- Keep each article's "今日小知识" around 300-500 Chinese characters unless the user asks otherwise.
- Use a different real story, person, place, route, object, or scene for each article. Do not reuse the same narrative pattern three times.
- Do not include "销售可转述话术".
- Do not include "产品 / 空间关联".
- Do not include "明日预告".
- Use attractive Markdown spacing, dividers, and tasteful emoji throughout.
- Use more emoji than a plain memo, but keep them refined and relevant.
- Each article's poster title must be a question.
- Each article's WeChat Moments copy must be short, premium, and elegant. It should answer the poster question through a concrete image, then naturally introduce how proper collecting/storage helps.
- Each article ends with its own Tags line inside the WeChat Moments section. Do not add a global summary after the third Tags line.

## Compliance Boundaries

- Do not claim health benefits for tea, alcohol, or cigars.
- Do not encourage smoking, drinking, overconsumption, or underage use.
- For cigars and alcohol, focus on culture, collection, storage, etiquette, craftsmanship, provenance, and responsible appreciation.
- Avoid medical, therapeutic, investment-return, or guaranteed appreciation claims.
